On the Purpose of this Dialogue

Philosophy begins not in lecture halls or treatises, but in the lived encounter with perplexity — the moment when what seemed obvious reveals itself as deeply uncertain. ΔιάΛογος is an attempt to hold open that moment of uncertainty, to inhabit it with patience and rigor.

Drawing from the long tradition of Western philosophy — from the pre-Socratics through Heidegger and Merleau-Ponty — and from the psychoanalytic inheritance of Freud, Lacan, and Jung, these writings explore the regions where thought and the unconscious overlap: where reason discovers its own limits, and where the irrational speaks a deeper truth.

The name ΔιάΛογος — dialogue — is chosen deliberately. Philosophy is never a monologue. It requires an interlocutor: sometimes a text, sometimes a tradition, sometimes the reader themselves. These articles are invitations, not pronouncements.
